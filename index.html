LPs as cooperation

Liquidity pools aren't just financial instruments — they're coordination primitives. When two token communities create a shared pool, they're choosing to connect their economies. Both sides provide liquidity, both earn fees on cross-traffic, and the routing connection itself creates value by making both tokens more accessible.

shared pools

Two communities each contribute to a pool connecting their tokens. Volume in either direction generates fees for both sides. The pool is shared infrastructure — not owned by either party, maintained by mutual interest.

hub routing

$ARBME sits between communities. Instead of needing a direct pair between every token, you route through the hub. Five connected tokens create ten routes. The mesh compounds — every new connection benefits every existing one.

gateway pools

Low-fee (0.05%) USDC entry points serve as shared infrastructure. They keep prices aligned, make entry and exit cheap, and drive traffic to the higher-fee pools where LPs earn. The gateways aren't profit centers — they're public goods for the ecosystem.

fee tiers as agreements

The fee tier on a pool is a statement about the relationship. Low fees say "this is infrastructure, come freely." High fees say "this is volatile, and we're both earning for taking on the risk." Matching the fee to the reality is how both sides stay aligned.

where we're going

More cooperation primitives. More connections between communities that want them. Better tooling for shared pool creation and management.

The model: position early, ride the full cycle, earn fees that benefit both sides. Token creators get countertrade volume. The hub earns routing fees. Both communities become more accessible. We're building for reciprocal value — not extraction.
